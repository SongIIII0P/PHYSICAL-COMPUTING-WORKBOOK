The first idea is called Emotional Shoes.
There’s a pressure sensor embedded in the sole,
every step triggers a sound that changes
based on how strong and fast the step is.

Because, well…our footsteps change with our mood, right?
It’s like expressing emotion through your walk.
If I use a pressure sensor and a speaker,
and set up different notes and delay in the code…
I think it could actually work...?
maybe?

The second idea is called Fragmented Mirror.
It’s more like an art installation I want to make.
It detects a person from different angles,
and shows a distorted version of themself on the monitor
based on the sensor data!

There’s a monitor with a built-in webcam
and it uses sensors like infrared distance sensors,
photoresistors, and temperature sensors to read the viewer.

The monitor captures and zooms in on parts of the person
like eyes, nose, mouth, hands, legs.
Depending on their temperature, movements,
or how close they are, the image shifts —
colours change, noise appears, things become distorted!

Of course, the coding wouldn’t be easy
but it could turn out pretty amazing, right?

function setup() {
createCanvas(windowWidth, windowHeight);
background(100);
textFont('Courier New');
textSize(20);

serial = new Serial();
serial.on(SerialEvents.CONNECTION_OPENED, onSerialConnectionOpened);
serial.on(SerialEvents.CONNECTION_CLOSED, onSerialConnectionClosed);
serial.on(SerialEvents.DATA_RECEIVED, onSerialDataReceived);
serial.on(SerialEvents.ERROR_OCCURRED, onSerialErrorOccurred);
msg = "Not connected";
}

function draw() {
let mappedData=map(dataIn, 0, 255, 0, height);
background(dataIn);
fill(0,255,0);
circle(width/2, height/2, mappedData);
text(msg,40,textY)
}

Seiko Mikami-Desire of Codes
https://www.ycam.jp/en/archive/works/desire-of-codes/

And one of my personal favourite works —
Seiko Mikami’s Desire of Codes.

This artwork uses motion tracking, cameras,
and mechanical sensors to follow the audience in real time.
The lights and cameras react to people’s movement and sound
They physically shift and track the body, almost like they’re alive!

What I really love is how the recorded footage gets broken up
and reassembled across 61 different screens.
The image becomes fragmented and scattered like digital shards.
It feels unsettling and slightly overwhelming
like you’re being watched and broken apart.

That definitely inspired my Fragmented Mirror!
